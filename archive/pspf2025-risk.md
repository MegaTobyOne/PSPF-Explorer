# PSPF 2025 Risk Domain Requirements

| ID | Title | Requirement Description |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 36 | Security Risk Tolerance | The entity documents and communicates its security risk tolerance as part of the security plan, outlining acceptable levels and management of risk. [^1] |
| 37 | Assign Risk Steward/Manager | A risk steward or manager is identified for each category of security risk impacting the entity. [^1] |
| 38 | Share Security Risk Info | The entity shares information about security risks where appropriate, supporting the management of shared, external, or government-wide risk impacts. [^1] |
| 39 | Procurements/Contracts Risk Management | Contracts, procurements, or service arrangements must not expose the entity to unacceptable security risk. [^1] |
| 40 | Contract Security Terms | Contract terms must include proportional and effective security provisions, and ensure compliance with the PSPF. [^1] |
| 41 | Provider PSPF Compliance | The entity ensures contracted providers comply with relevant PSPF requirements. [^1] |
| 42 | Incident Reporting in Contracts | Contracts mandate incident reporting and compliance with directions. [^1] |
| 43 | Outsourced/Cloud IRAP Reports | Outsourced or third-party providers, including cloud services, must supply ASD IRAP security assessment reports. [^1] |
| 44 | Monitor Contract Compliance | Contractual terms and provider performance are monitored/reviewed periodically to confirm effective security controls and compliance. [^1] |
| 45 | Contract Completion/Termination Security | Contracts include arrangements for the completion and secure termination/destroying of sensitive information. [^1] |
| 46 | Foreign Ownership/Control/Influence Risks | Security risk of foreign ownership, control, or influence in providers must be assessed before and during contract. [^1] |
| 47 | Ongoing Contract Risk Management | Risks associated with third-party contracts are actively managed and reassessed throughout the lifecycle. [^1] |
| 48 | Secure Vendor Selection | Third-party vendors/providers must be secure and verifiable by default. [^1] |
| 49 | Vendor Risk Management | Security risks are evaluated and managed when engaging vendors and suppliers. [^1] |
| 50 | Foreign Interference Program | The entity establishes and maintains an insider threat and foreign interference program as part of risk management. [^1] |
| 51 | Insider Threat Mitigation | Measures and monitoring are in place to detect, assess and address potential insider threats. [^1] |
| 52 | Contingency Planning | Entities must establish contingency plans for security incidents and business continuity. [^1] |
| 53 | Business Continuity Planning | Business continuity plans are maintained and regularly tested to mitigate disruptions to security and operations. [^1] |
| 54 | Critical Incident Management | Entities have structures and procedures in place for critical incident management, escalation, and response. [^1] |
| 55 | Emergency Management | Emergency management arrangements cover security impacts to people, assets, and information. [^1] |
| 56 | Threat and Vulnerability Assessments | Entities conduct ongoing threat and vulnerability assessments as part of risk management. [^1] |
| 57 | Shared or Whole-of-Gov Risks | Shared risks affecting other entities or government-wide interests are managed and communicated appropriately. [^1] |
