# PSPF 2025 Information Domain Requirements

| ID | Title | Requirement Description |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| 58 | Originator Control of Information | The originator remains responsible for controlling the sanitisation, reclassification or declassification of official and security classified information, and approves any changes to the information’s security classification. [^1] |
| 59 | Value/Sensitivity Assessment | The value, importance or sensitivity of official information intended for use as an official record is assessed by the originator by considering the potential damage to government, the national interest, organisations or individuals if confidentiality is compromised. [^1] |
| 60 | Lowest Reasonable Classification | The security classification is set at the lowest reasonable level. [^1] |
| 61 | Marking Classified Information | Security classified information is clearly marked with the applicable security classification, and when relevant, security caveat, by using text-based markings, unless impractical for operational reasons. [^1] |
| 62 | Minimum Protections Applied | The minimum protections and handling requirements are applied to protect OFFICIAL and security classified information. [^1] |
| 63 | Caveat Standard and Special Handling | The Australian Government Security Caveat Standard and special handling requirements imposed by the controlling authority are applied to protect security caveated information. [^1] |
| 64 | Text-Based Caveat Marking | Security caveats are clearly marked as text and only appear in conjunction with a security classification. [^1] |
| 65 | Accountable Material Marking | Accountable material has page and reference numbering. [^1] |
| 66 | Handling Accountable Material | Accountable material is handled in accordance with any special handling requirements imposed by the originator and security caveat owner detailed in the Australian Government Security Caveat Standard. [^1] |
| 67 | Email Protective Marking | The Australian Government Email Protective Marking Standard is applied to protect OFFICIAL and security classified information exchanged by email in and between Australian Government entities, including other authorised parties. [^1] |
| 68 | Metadata Standards Applied | The Australian Government Recordkeeping Metadata Standards Security Classification property and, where relevant, the Security Caveat property, is applied to protectively mark information on technology systems that store, process or communicate security classified information. [^1] |
| 69 | Rights Property on Content | Apply the Australian Government Recordkeeping Metadata Standards Rights property where the entity wishes to categorise information content by the type of restrictions on access. [^1] |
| 70 | Approved Locations for Classified Info | Security classified discussions and dissemination of security classified information are only conducted in approved locations. [^1] |
| 71 | Operational Controls for Holdings | Entity implements operational controls for its information holdings that are proportional to their value, importance and sensitivity. [^1] |
| 72 | Register for TOP SECRET Info | An auditable register is maintained for TOP SECRET information and accountable material. [^1] |
| 73 | Secure Disposal of Information | OFFICIAL and security classified information is disposed of securely in accordance with the Minimum Protections and Handling Requirements, Information Security Manual, the Records Authorities, a Normal Administrative Practice and the Archives Act 1983. [^1] |
| 74 | Destruction of Classified Information | Security classified information is appropriately destroyed in accordance with the Minimum Protections and Handling Requirements when it has passed the minimum retention requirements or reaches authorised destruction dates. [^1] |
| 75 | Access Control for Classified Info | Access to security classified information or resources is only provided to people outside the entity with the appropriate security clearance where required and a need-to-know, and is transferred in accordance with the Minimum Protections and Handling Requirements. [^1] |
| 76 | National MOU for Information Sharing | The Memorandum of Understanding between the Commonwealth, States and Territories is applied when sharing information with state and territory government agencies. [^1] |
| 77 | Agreements for Third-Party Sharing | An agreement or arrangement, such as a contract or deed, that establishes handling requirements and protections, is in place before security classified information or resources are disclosed or shared with a person or organisation outside of government (except where returning/responding to information they originally provided and which government subsequently classified as OFFICIAL:SENSITIVE). [^1] |
| 78 | International Agreement Protections | Provisions are met concerning the security of people, information and resources contained in international agreements and arrangements to which Australia is a party. [^1] |
| 79 | Foreign Entity Protections | Australian Government security classified information or resources shared with a foreign entity is protected by explicit legislative provision, international agreement or international arrangement. [^1] |
| 80 | Australian Eyes Only (AUSTEO) Checks | Australian Government security classified information or resources bearing the Australian Eyes Only (AUSTEO) caveat is never shared with a person who is not an Australian citizen, even when an international agreement or international arrangement is in place, unless exemption is granted. [^1] |
| 81 | Australian Government Access Only (AGAO) Caveat | Australian Government security classified information or resources bearing the Australian Government Access Only (AGAO) caveat is not shared with a person who is not an Australian citizen, except under specific departmental relationships or exemptions. [^1] |
| 82 | Foreign Entity Caveat Handling | Where an international agreement or arrangement is in place, security classified foreign entity information or resources are safeguarded by the provisions set out in the agreement or arrangement. [^1] |
| 83 | Non-government Stakeholder Protections | Australian Government security classified information or resources shared with a foreign non-government stakeholder is protected by explicit legislative provision, international agreement or international arrangement. [^1] |
