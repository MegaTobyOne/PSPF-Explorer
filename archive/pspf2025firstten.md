# Governance Domain

### Summary

Sets leadership structure, accountability, and planning for protective security across government entities.

### Explanation

The first 12 requirements establish core responsibilities (Accountable Authority, CSO, CISO), reporting lines, supporting roles, the existence of security plans, and the professional standards needed for key officers. They lay the foundation for the compliance culture and practical operation of protective security in the public sector.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Department of State supports portfolio entities with security advice.[^1]
2. Accountable Authority complies with all security directions.[^1]
3. Technical Authority Entity provides security advice.[^1]
4. Shared Service Provider Entity supplies security services.[^1]
5. Documented responsibilities/accountabilities for partnerships or service arrangements.[^1]
6. Accountable Authority is answerable to Minister for security.[^1]
7. Accountable Authority manages security risks for the entity.[^1]
8. Appoint Chief Security Officer.[^1]
9. CSO is SES level and holds minimum 'Negative Vetting 1' clearance.[^1]
10. CSO is accountable to Accountable Authority for security matters.[^1]
11. Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) oversees cyber program and critical technology resources.[^1]
12. CISO must have capability plus minimum 'Negative Vetting 1' clearance.[^1]

- **Measures:** Security plan development, senior executive appointment, formal reporting structures, clearance checks, training access.

# Risk Domain

### Summary

Requires entities to identify, evaluate, and manage security risks holistically, including supply chain, shared risks, and third-party dependencies.

### Explanation

Requirements define risk stewardship, cross-entity risk impact, vendor risk controls, and contract measures. They require robust procurement governance and continuous risk reassessment over lifespan of contracts and partnerships.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Document risk tolerance in entity security plan.[^1]
2. Assign risk steward/manager for each category of security risk.[^1]
3. Share security risk information where appropriate.[^1]
4. Entities must manage risks from procurement and contracts.[^1]
5. Contracts must have security terms and ensure providers follow the PSPF.[^1]
6. Ensure providers comply with relevant PSPF requirements.[^1]
7. Contract terms mandate incident reporting and direction compliance.[^1]
8. Outsourced providers must provide IRAP assessment reports.[^1]
9. Monitor/review contract terms to ensure security controls are working.[^1]
10. Contracts must include completion/termination security arrangements.[^1]
11. Foreign ownership/control risks must be addressed before and during contracts.[^1]
12. Continually manage and reassess third-party contract risks.[^1]

- **Measures:** Risk documentation, periodic contract review, procurement guidelines, incident reporting protocols.

# Information Domain

### Summary

Mandates protective marking, classification, caveating, and secure documentation practices for government information.

### Explanation

Requirements ensure all official and classified info is labeled, managed, disposed of securely, and shared only under robust agreements or conditions. They put stringent protocols around information handling and sharing, particularly with external and international parties.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Originator controls sanitisation/reclassification/declassification of information.[^1]
2. Value/sensitivity of official info assessed by originator.[^1]
3. Set classification at lowest reasonable level.[^1]
4. Security-classified info is marked with classification/caveat where relevant.[^1]
5. Apply minimum protections to OFFICIAL and classified info.[^1]
6. Security caveats and special handling per controlling authority.[^1]
7. Security caveats marked as text, only with classification.[^1]
8. Accountable material must have page/reference numbering.[^1]
9. Accountable material handled under relevant caveat/handling rules.[^1]
10. Email marking standard applies when exchanging info.[^1]
11. Recordkeeping metadata standards for security marking on systems.[^1]
12. Metadata property applied for information access restrictions.[^1]

- **Measures:** Protective markings, recordkeeping standards, controlled disposal, access agreements.

# Technology Domain

### Summary

Focuses on approval, authorisation, and monitoring of tech assets, cyber strategy, and use of certified hosting and cloud services.

### Explanation

Requirements cover risk-based controls for technology, patching and hardening, gateway controls, cloud service provider standards, and cyber uplift measures, stressing Zero Trust and Essential Eight maturity for cyber hygiene.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Apply cyber security principles throughout technology lifecycle.[^1]
2. ASD ISM controls/guidelines applied on risk basis.[^1]
3. Technology Asset Stocktake and Tech Security Risk Management Plan for internet-facing systems.[^1]
4. Authorise each tech system to operate based on risk acceptance.[^1]
5. Authorisation decisions based on ISM risk-based assessment.[^1]
6. System authorised to its highest data classification.[^1]
7. Register of authorised tech systems maintained.[^1]
8. Reassess system suitability on significant changes.[^1]
9. TikTok application blocked on government devices (with strict mitigations if required).[^1]
10. Temporary mitigations applied for legacy IT.[^1]
11. Store tech assets in security zones by classification.[^1]
12. Top Secret IT assets stored in SCEC/ASIO-prescribed secure racks/compartments.[^1]

- **Measures:** Technology stocktakes, system registration, IRAP/ISM adherence, periodic risk reviews, mandatory patching.

# Personnel Domain

### Summary

Details pre-employment screening, suitability, clearance process, managing temporary access, and risk management for personnel.

### Explanation

First 12 requirements highlight thorough checks before access, confirmation of eligibility/suitability, temporary access restrictions, and need-to-know protocols. There is a strong focus on security clearance integrity and limiting access risk.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Ensure eligibility and suitability of all personnel.[^1]
2. Conduct identity check for all staff (Level 3 Assurance).[^1]
3. Verify biographic info against original identity documents.[^1]
4. Confirm work eligibility and AG eligibility for staff.[^1]
5. Obtain personnel assurance/agreement to comply with relevant standards.[^1]
6. Temporary access only after checks/clearance for TOP SECRET info.[^1]
7. Temporary access based on risk assessment.[^1]
8. Supervised temporary access to classified info/resources.[^1]
9. Temporary access is time-limited to 3 months max in most cases.[^1]
10. Vetting agency confirms clearance pack before provisional access.[^1]
11. Temporary access to caveated info only in exceptional circumstances.[^1]
12. Personnel must sign an undertaking to protect info/resources before temporary access.[^1]

- **Measures:** Screening, clearance confirmation, signed undertakings, access time limits.

***

# Physical Domain

### Summary

Covers planning, construction, operation, certification, access control, and protective measures for secure facilities and physical assets.

### Explanation

Initial requirements mandate facility security planning, site selection, secure construction by risk level, and certified/accredited use of security zones. Emphasis is placed on controls for doors, alarms, guards, and asset protection by impact level.[^1]

### Requirements \& Measures

1. Integrate protective security planning/design of facilities.[^1]
2. Facility security plans for new/modified buildings.[^1]
3. Site location considers security risk factors.[^1]
4. Secure facility access commensurate with risk.[^1]
5. Construction meets technical notes for public/authorised areas/tech assets.[^1]
6. Security Zones 2-5 built per ASIO Tech Notes for classified assets.[^1]
7. Operate/maintain facilities per Security Zones and controls.[^1]
8. Zones 1-4 certified before operational use.[^1]
9. Zone 5 (TOP SECRET) certified by ASIO-T4 before use.[^1]
10. Zones 1-5 accredited before use, ensuring correct controls and accepted risks.[^1]
11. Sensitive Compartmented Information Facility areas for TOP SECRET accredited by ASD before use.[^1]
12. Physical security measures prevent unauthorised access/use/removal of assets.[^1]

- **Measures:** Site security plans, certification, technical note compliance, layered access, asset protection.
